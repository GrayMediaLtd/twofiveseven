our space

with over a decade of community building and spacemaking in Rotterdam, Vienna, Whangārei and now Whanganui-a-Tara, spacelamp is driving the creation of our space.

with a range of spaces to suit the needs of you and your team:

  1. permanent desks
  2. flexible desks
  3. meeting rooms
  4. meetup booths
  5. large event space
  6. space (rent by the day + full clean between users)
  7. cafe seating
  8. grandstand seating
  9. secluded space for breaks

onsite we will have a cafe, workspace kitchen, and aotearoa’s only mental health gym by CoLiberate

we’re using walls from local circular economy heroes x-frame, and uses much of the spaces previous fitout in the making of our new space

with covid responsive design features from experienced lab designers studio pacific architecture, brand new ventilation systems, regular deep cleaning from impact driven cleaners fresh desk, and our locally made bespoke hand sanitiser supply from ofbody - our space will be one of the first to respond to the new world of infection control

spacelamp is certified carbon positive for 2019 by ekos. twofiveseven aims to achieve the same rating for construction and operations once the respective data is available - we are doing everything we can do to have a sustainable impact on our world

when operational, twofiveseven will be living wage accredited - helping you and your organisation maintain or develop your own certification if you use our space

we are planning to be open in november 2020

our full office

our full office space will consist of 15 work desks, 2 meetup booths, 2 cafe tables and the option of access to 1 or two large meeting rooms - bookable just for you and your team

this space is rentable by the day - and given a deep clean between uses.

if your team is working remotely and just needs to get together once a week, month, quarter or at whatever schedule suits you - we got you covered

our furniture

growing out of the resource rise again project by rekindle, local furniture designers and manufacturers designtree are creating our custom furniture range from materials salvaged from the old, 1980’s office fitout that was previously in the space

this means we’re able to dramatically reduce waste and our carbon footprint in creating our space - something we are striving for across all of our construction and development

it also has enabled us to develop a furniture system directly responsive to a world in which work is rapidly changing. we’ve partnered with local research startup stickybeak to define the primary needs of local office users, now that working remotely, using video call software frequently, and distance meeting are all our new normal

our desks offer sit and stand positions, and come with an optional full privacy guard so you are able to safely and comfortably video call and physical distance at your desk

we are also offering a rental service for our desks at home. with the coming shift to a mix of working from both home and the office, we think having the same desks for you and your team at both the office and home will make the transitions a whole lot easier

our story

twofiveseven is being led by spacelamp - a community building, spacemaking and impact creating organisation with over a decade of experience in the netherlands, austria and aotearoa

most recently we’ve created and developed spaces and community hubs in whangārei:

oneonesix is a project by company of giants, with support from spacelamp, turning an unused former church in the centre of whangārei into a thriving hub for community events, theatre, music and art. the space now has over 13,000 annually, is home to a community radio station, small coffee bar, and hosts a huge and diverse range of events throughout the year

wood was an unused warehouse space and we were a part of turning it into a space that has supported over 20 community organisations, startups and impact organisations. today it is home to forward, ano reuse again, sew good, wholefood hub, and whare bike

we’ve since moved to whanganui-a-tara, and currently our major focus is the twofiveseven space
